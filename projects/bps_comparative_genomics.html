Comparative Genomics of Burkholderia pseudomallei

Whole-genome sequencing and relapse-specific variant analysis in melioidosis

Bacterial genomics WGS Comparative genomics Variant analysis

Genomics workflow

End-to-end analysis from clinical isolates to functional interpretation of relapse-associated variants.

Background & Motivation

Burkholderia pseudomallei is the causative agent of melioidosis, a severe infectious disease endemic to South and Southeast Asia. During my research at JIPMER, we investigated a relapse case in a diabetic patient despite completion of recommended antimicrobial therapy.

Research questions

  • Are there genomic differences between primary and relapse isolates?
  • Do mutations or structural variants explain persistence or relapse?
  • Are virulence-associated loci altered?

Data & Methods

Samples & sequencing

  • Two clinical isolates: primary infection (EX14915) and relapse (EX7035)
  • Illumina NextSeq 500, paired-end 2×150 bp (~100× coverage)

Bioinformatics tools

  • QC & assembly: FastQC, Shovill
  • Annotation: Prokka, PubMLST
  • Comparative genomics: Mash, BRIG
  • Orthologs: OrthoVenn3
  • Variants: Snippy, TIDDIT
  • Functional analysis: STRING, GO enrichment

Key Results

  • Primary and relapse isolates were highly similar at the cgMLST level
  • Very few non-synonymous SNPs accumulated over 9 months
  • Multiple structural variants were detected in the relapse isolate
  • Functional enrichment highlighted stress-response and adaptation pathways

Reproducibility & Data

  • NCBI BioProject: PRJNA781752
  • All analyses performed using default parameters unless stated
