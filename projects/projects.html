Within‑host evolution of Burkholderia pseudomallei

Comparative genomics of paired clinical isolates collected six months apart from a single patient

I performed a high‑resolution comparative genomic analysis of two B. pseudomallei isolates obtained from the same patient six months apart. Despite prolonged infection, only a handful of mutations were observed, suggesting strong within‑host genomic stability. Network‑based functional analyses revealed isolate‑specific enrichment of key virulence‑associated genes.

  • Identified extremely low within‑host mutation rates during chronic infection.
  • Inferred isolate‑specific virulence determinants using functional interaction networks.
  • Provided insights into persistence mechanisms of B. pseudomallei in human hosts.
Comparative genomics Bacterial WGS GO / network analysis Python R

Publication: Journal of Medical Microbiology (2023)

Core‑genome AMR landscape of pneumococcal GPSC lineages in India

Population‑scale genomic analysis of Streptococcus pneumoniae isolates

Using whole‑genome sequencing data from Indian pneumococcal isolates, I analysed core genomes in the context of Global Pneumococcal Sequence Clusters (GPSCs). The study revealed that GPSC10 harbours a conserved core set of antimicrobial resistance genes, pointing to widespread and stable AMR within this lineage.

  • Mapped resistance determinants within lineage‑specific core genomes.
  • Highlighted GPSC10 as a high‑risk genotype with pervasive AMR.
  • Provided population‑level evidence relevant to surveillance and vaccine policy.
Population genomics AMR analysis Phylogenetics Pan‑/core‑genome

Preprint: bioRxiv (2024)

Interpretable modelling of drug response using joint matrix factorisation

Linking gene expression, dependency, and drug sensitivity in cancer cell lines

I developed a joint matrix factorisation framework to model gene expression, gene dependency, and drug response simultaneously. By deliberately using simple linear components, the model remains interpretable while still capturing biologically meaningful latent structure. This enabled identification of key genes and pathways driving resistance to targeted therapies such as EGFR inhibitors.

  • Proposed an interpretable alternative to deep learning for drug response prediction.
  • Derived gene‑level benefit scores linking dependency and IC50 responses.
  • Recovered known resistance mechanisms and suggested novel candidate drivers.
